It sounds incredibly frustrating to be managing two separate dinner menus while also worrying about your partner's declining health. It's completely valid that you want an equal partnership rather than feeling like a caregiver.

To approach this without sounding maternal, focus the conversation on *your* feelings and boundaries rather than *his* diet. Try using "I" statements, such as: "I am exhausted cooking two meals every night, and I need us to find a sustainable solution together." You can express concern for his health, but ultimately, his diet is his responsibility. You might need to establish a boundary where you cook one meal, and if he prefers something else, he is responsible for preparing it himself.
